The Progression of Education

Introduction

Education is the bases of all things for knowledge gives us the power to do anything we wish to do. And like technology, education progresses and transforms evolving and growing. Just over a hundred years ago education was not something most countries of the world offered to all citizens. Even today not everyone in the world has access to education, but that is ever changing as does education and technology.

VR technology has the potential to revolutionize education in many ways. Whether it is used in the classroom to add virtual worlds upon what you see or transport you across the world. In the future it may even give everyone in the world access to education. "Using Virtual Reality in Education", an article by eLearning Industry stated that Google Cardboard is a cheap VR option that can be used with cheap Android phones, which opens the door for VR to be used in education.2 In fact Google has been giving test kits to schools to try out VR in education.1 Tech Crunch's article "When Virtual Reality Meets Education" talks about what this kit includes being: ASUS smart phones, Google Cardboard headsets, and 100+ VR tours for use in education.1

Conclusion

Education is the beginning of our lives as humans. The first thing we do when we are born is start to learn from those around us. Then we enter a system of education which tries to prepare us for the working world. VR and AR technology have the opportunity to forever change the way we learn. It may begin with helping to explain abstract concepts and ideas or to work with the different learning styles of students. It may have visual tours or interactive games.

In the end education could come to a point where everyone no longer goes to physical schools but stay home where they use their VR headsets. However we must remember that there are times technology should not be used in education. If the teachers are not using VR correctly or don't know how to use it can be detrimental to learning. The same situation as over using the technology and becoming dependent upon it. And we must be weary not to use the technology too earlier in the education of students.

Too many video games, too much computer time, and sitting too close to the television can be harmful to youth. The same could be said of VR technology. It may be an incredible tool but we must use it carefully. I argue that VR technology would be helpful for students in high school and college but not so much for elementary or middle school.

Too much progress too fast can be an issue, but today is the time to start bringing VR and AR to the classroom. Everything we can do to improve learning must be done to help the next generation to be better than the last. Though we must be cautious of how we use it in the classroom. Today we take the first step, tomorrow we take another as Education slowly transforms.
