Far Reaches of Education

To peer into the past and watch the world form, wars be won and lost, empires rise and fall, and civil rights movements. See Neil Armstrong land on the moon or the far corners of space. Class unbound with students from across the world talking together in the same room. VR has a far reach and education can take advantage of it to expand the learning of students around the world. The virtual classroom offers many experience we could only dream of having, today it may be just out of reach but soon it may be part of every student's education.

Virtual classroom's offer a new experience that breaks down the original physical bounds that we once abided by. This could be any number of virtual tours or talking with foreign students. If students got opportunities to talk to other students from other countries it would be a new experience. It could lead to new friendships and bonds between countries. Not to mention opportunities for students to further their foreign language skills. Imagine if learning another language was done by talking with those who actually know that language.

VR Educational tours can come in many forms. It may be walking across a historic war ship, through 1930s New York City, or even watch battles from Roman wars. eLearning Industry discusses this ideas as being panoramic tours using text to help describe the seen.1 It isn't just a set of pictures but a world, you can walk around or interact with the world. Students would even be able to linger in a spot and learn more about things they are interested in.1

A joy of learning can be hard to instill with stale movies, long lectures, or hard to read books. But with a VR world that you can use to focus in on key things that you enjoy, school can inspire students to want to learn. One student may go to learn about the engineering of a plane while another wants to look at the controls and how you would fly it. One company working on such tours and VR technology is Roundme which works well with Google Cardboard.1

The far reaches of education is not just how technology can be used to improve learning. It is not just about the things that we can learn about and how we take charge of our learning. It is also about motivating students to learn, to encourage and inspire the next generation. The best way to do this is to bring education into a brave new world of VR and AR technology.
