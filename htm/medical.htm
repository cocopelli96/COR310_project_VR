Medical VR

There are two parts to the medical field I would like to discuss being physical and mental. Both surgical medical work and psychological training can be improved by the use of VR technology. VR can introduce interesting new concepts to the medical field from streaming surgeries and interactive training environments to possible augmented reality glassess that can pull up medical history while the doctor looks at their patient. One such use is for the rehabilitation of patients.

A SteamFeed article '5 Ways Virtual Reality is Transforming Existing Business Model', states that rehabilitation of physically disabled patients is a great use for VR technology as it can create intersting environments to help regain motor skills.2 It can also be used for rehabilitation of those with mental issues such as PTSD, with different simulations.2 Along the same line, those patients with autism can benefit from VR to work on social skills in a more open and friendly environment.2

Anxiety is another big issue which is a medical problem and in ways also a psychological problem. One study by french psychologists has already worked to see the effect of VR training on performance anxiety.1 They used a CAVE system of VR inorder to immerse the performance in a fake audience.1 The performers didn't actual interact with the audience but instead simply played music. Essentially this was exposure training for the performers. The study used two groups of students who were to perform in two recitals before and after the VR training.1

The first group was a control group which did not get any VR exposure and simply trained as they normally did before a recital.1 The second group was exposed to the VR training with six session in the VR environment.1 The study had mixed results in a way. It is more helpful for those with high anxiety rather than low anxiety, which didn't seem to have much improvement.1 Music performance anxiety is not the only thing this type of VR training might be able to help with. People have fears and anxiety in all sort of situations and this type of medical exposure training could help to make people more comfortable in adverse situations.
