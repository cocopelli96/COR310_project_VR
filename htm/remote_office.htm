The Remote Office

In today's world the need to be in the same room as those you are working with is ever increasing.2 This may sound strange in a world with more and more technology which allows people to interact at further and further distances. Yet it is a fact that we need to be able to work together better and the issue can be trying to determine exactly what others are doing when you are not in the same room. VR has the potential to revolutionize the business world by reducing the distance between people, in a similar way to the how teleconferences changed the business world. VR can transfer the need of being in the same room from the real world to the virtual world.

The article 'How Virtual Reality Will Change Life In The Workplace' from Forbes talks about using VR technology to create a virtual workplace, a digital replica which you can enter into with an avatar.1 In this way you have the ability to converse in a room as if you were all physically there. It is an update to the concept of a teleconference and the beginning of the completely digital office.1 Imagine that instead of commuting to work every day you just put on a sort of headset which allows you to enter a virtual workplace. Maybe this is headset is VR placing you completely in the virtual world, and maybe it is AR allowing you to communicate with other works while still working in your home.

The remote office is the idea that you no longer need to be around to work together, the office you work in is not the main office. The blog ipswitch has a small article 'The Future of Virtual Reality in the Workplace' which looks at another form of the remote workplace, using VR to control robots instead of doing VR teleconferences.2 Some believe that the future of the workplace replaces the real body with a robot doppelganger which we control from our homes using VR technology.2 Luckily though with no control software or A.I. these robots would not pose the Terminator threat.2

But is the remote office a good thing? We are talking about technology that makes it so you never have to be sick again. Who misses a meeting when they're sick, if they can put a headset on and make the virtual meeting?2 Removing the need to go into the office, increases the ability of employers to take advantage of their employees demanding that they work more because they no longer need to commute. Though there may be many benefits for employees who no longer need to commute to the office, the fact that employers could take advantage of this is a horrifying idea which begins simply with the employee being capable of working from anywhere.
